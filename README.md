# Connected but Left Behind
### Public Transport Accessibility and Unemployment Across English LSOAs

**Module:** CASA0006 — Data Science for Spatial Systems  
**Programme:** MSc Urban Spatial Science, CASA, UCL  
**Author:** Nadia Jimena Cabrera Salazar  

---

## Overview

This project investigates whether public transport job accessibility predicts lower unemployment across 31,810 English Lower Super Output Areas (LSOAs), and under what structural conditions that relationship breaks down.

Using XGBoost and SHAP analysis, the study finds that structural conditions (car-deprivation, overcrowding, lone parenthood, education) overwhelmingly dominate prediction, while PT accessibility ranks 21st of 27 features. The relationship breaks down most severely in deprived urban communities, where PT and unemployment co-locate through shared structural geography rather than causal connection.
